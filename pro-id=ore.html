PRODUCT DESIGN

Ọrẹ Mi

Labels & Identity — Voice Assistant Device Bridging AI and Social Media

PARTNERSHIP indstndrd [now ether]
CATEGORY Creative Direction / Cultural Strategy
DELIVERABLES Editorial Series, Visual Campaign, Copy & Concept Development
TOOLS Adobe Creative Suite

01

UNDERSTANDING

Ọrẹ Mi is an exploration of what it means to name yourself in a world that's always trying to do it for you. Inspired by graffiti culture and the Yoruba phrase Ore Mi ("my friend"), this series examines how the labels we wear become markers of the space we claim, temporary, evolving, and never the full story.

02

DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

No one fits into a neat category, and this project doesn't try to force it. The design approach behind Ọrẹ Mi mirrors the concept itself: fluid, layered, and intentional. Every visual choice was guided by the idea that a label is a starting point, not a conclusion. Just as identity shifts with experience, the aesthetic was built to feel like something in motion, a declaration that's honest about its own incompleteness. The goal wasn't to define, but to open a conversation about what definition even means.

03

GRAFFITI AS INFLUENCE

Graffiti doesn't ask for permission. That's the point. Whether it's a quick tag or a full piece, every mark is an act of presence, proof that someone was here, that they took up space in a world that didn't necessarily invite them in. As West Philly graffiti legend Kool Klepto Kidd put it, "Wherever there was a little space in there, we would write our names. It was almost like we were here like it was our route."

That energy is the backbone of this series. Graffiti isn't decoration, it's a claim. And just like a tag on a wall, the labels we wear are temporary. They exist for a moment, shift with the story, and make way for what comes next. Ọrẹ Mi borrows that same ethos: mark where you are, know it won't last forever, and keep moving.

01

Allude

Google & X (Twitter)

Voice Assistant Device Bridging AI and Social Media Interaction

OVERVIEW

Allude is a voice assistant device that bridges AI and social media in a portable, social-native format. Inspired by the Magic 8 Ball, it transforms how we interact with digital content by making the familiar gesture of shaking a sophisticated interface for accessing both personalized social insights and factual information.

THE OPPORTUNITY

Despite 4.9 billion social media users globally, interaction remains screen-dependent. Gen-Z and Millennials expect seamless tech integration, but current AI assistants don't speak the language of social platforms. Existing solutions force users to choose between scrolling feeds or voice assistance, never both simultaneously.

02

Heated

IKEA & Adobe

Biophilic Candle Warmer, Lamp & Incense Holder Designed for Everyday Creative Living

OVERVIEW

A mushroom-form lamp, candle warmer and incense holder that brings biophilic calm and smart ambiance control into everyday domestic life. Designed at the intersection of IKEA's democratic design philosophy and Adobe's creative empowerment ethos, Heated engineers atmosphere as a functional outcome, using warmth, fragrance, and biophilic form to shift the sensory conditions of a room and the cognitive state of the person in it.

THE OPPORTUNITY

The home has always been a space for rest. But rest and creativity aren't opposites; the right environment produces both. Research consistently shows that sensory cues shape cognitive state: ambient warmth shifts people toward intuitive, experiential thinking; pleasant fragrance improves mood and creative task performance; nature-adjacent forms reduce mental fatigue and restore directed attention. Most home objects address one of these levers at a time. Heated addresses all three simultaneously.

03

Ọrẹ Mi

Labels & Identity

What labels do you wear?

OVERVIEW

Ọrẹ Mi is an exploration of what it means to name yourself in a world that's always trying to do it for you. Inspired by graffiti culture and the Yoruba phrase Ore Mi ("my friends"), this series examines how the labels we wear become markers of the space we claim — temporary, evolving, and never the full story.

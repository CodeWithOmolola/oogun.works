Kodo

Premium Japanese Incense & Wellness Brand Honoring Traditional Craftsmanship

CATEGORY Wellness & Lifestyle Brand
DELIVERABLES Brand Identity, Packaging Design, Product Line Strategy, Market Research
TOOLS Adobe Creative Suite, Market Research

01

OVERVIEW

The global wellness market is projected to reach $9.8 trillion by 2029, with incense and aromatherapy as a leading sector. However, most culturally derived aesthetic brands produce products without cultural understanding or philosophical traditions that make these practices meaningful.

Global Wellness Institute, 2025 Global Wellness Economy Monitor


Kodo is a premium wellness brand rooted in the Japanese concept of 間 (ma)—the meaningful space between moments. Our incense and fragrance oils honor centuries of Japanese incense ceremonies and traditional craftsmanship, creating products that invite mindful presence rather than passive consumption. Kodo builds authentic relationships with artisans, sources materials ethically, and educates consumers about the cultural significance behind each offering.

02

STRATEGIC FOUNDATION

Target Audience

Culturally curious consumers (28-45) who value authenticity over trends. They research brands, appreciate craftsmanship, and understand that premium pricing reflects ethical sourcing and artisan partnerships. They view wellness as practice, not product.

"Gen Zers and millennials make up just over a third (36 percent) of the adult population in the United States, they drive more than 41 percent of annual wellness spend.""

McKinsey & Company, Future of Wellness Trends University

Brand Positioning

Kodo positions at the intersection of cultural preservation and modern wellness. Not a "Japanese-inspired" brand, but a brand that honors Japanese tradition through collaboration, education, and respect. Premium without being pretentious. Accessible without being appropriative.

04

DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

Visual Identity

The packaging honors traditional Japanese design principles: restraint, natural materials, and meaningful negative space. Cherry blossom illustrations are hand-drawn by Japanese botanical artist, not stock imagery. Typography balances Japanese characters (ケロワロ). Color palette drawn from natural materials, blush pink of sakura, deep forest green of hinoki, warm beige of temple wood.

Materials

Packaging uses FSC-certified paper and soy-based inks. No plastic shrink wrap. Boxes designed to be kept and reused, beautiful enough to display, functional enough to store jewelry, notes, keepsakes. This extends product lifecycle and reduces waste while honoring Japanese concept of mottainai (regret over waste).

Cultural Education

Every product includes context card explaining: historical significance of the scent, traditional uses in Japanese culture, proper burning techniques for incense ceremony (kōdō), and stories of the artisans who crafted it. This transforms purchase into learning experience, building respect rather than appropriation.

05

GO-TO-MARKET

Launch Strategy

Direct-to-consumer launch via Shopify with editorial content strategy. Partner with wellness influencers who value authenticity. Launch during cherry blossom season with limited-edition sakura collection.

Distribution

Year 1: DTC only, building brand story and community.
Year 2: Selective retail: boutique wellness stores, museum gift shops (Japanese American National Museum, MoMA), high-end home goods retailers. Avoid mass market to maintain premium positioning and cultural respect.

Content Marketing

Documentary-style videos featuring partner artisans in Japan (Split Short Content for TikTok). Blog content exploring Japanese aesthetic philosophy. Instagram posts of product beauty with educational copy. Email Newsletters teaching incense ceremony basics.
